<h2>Summary (EN)</h2>
<p><strong>Agile</strong> is an <em>iterative &amp; incremental</em> way of working focused on <strong>value</strong>, <strong>fast feedback</strong>, and <strong>collaboration</strong>. Use it when requirements are volatile and you need early value; for heavy compliance or fixed scope, consider <strong>Hybrid</strong> or <strong>Predictive</strong>.</p>

<h2>1) What is Agile?</h2>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Agile</strong> is a <strong>project management philosophy</strong> centered on a <strong>flexible response to change</strong>.</li>
  <li>Instead of a fixed plan, Agile <strong>breaks work down</strong> into short <strong>Sprints</strong>.</li>
  <li>Each Sprint produces an <strong>Increment</strong>‚Äîa <strong>working</strong>, <strong>value-delivering</strong> product slice.</li>
  <li><em>In short:</em> <strong>Small batches ‚Äì early delivery ‚Äì fast learning ‚Äì continuous adaptation</strong>.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Agile Manifesto: 4 Core Values</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Individuals &amp; interactions</strong> <em>over</em> processes &amp; tools.</li>
  <li><strong>Working product</strong> <em>over</em> comprehensive documentation.</li>
  <li><strong>Customer collaboration</strong> <em>over</em> contract negotiation.</li>
  <li><strong>Responding to change</strong> <em>over</em> following a plan.</li>
  <li>Items on the left are <strong>valued more</strong> (not a rejection of the right).</li>
</ul>

<h3>Key Terms</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Sprint:</strong> a fixed timebox for <strong>inspection</strong> &amp; <strong>rapid adjustment</strong>.</li>
  <li><strong>Increment:</strong> a <strong>shippable/usable</strong> product slice at the end of each Sprint.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Prominent Agile Methods</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Scrum</strong>, <strong>Kanban</strong>, <strong>XP</strong> (eXtreme Programming), <strong>Lean</strong>.</li>
  <li>Operate via <strong>transparency ‚Üí inspection ‚Üí adaptation</strong> for <strong>continuous improvement</strong>.</li>
</ul>

<h2>2) When to use / not use Agile</h2>

<h3>Use Agile when</h3>
<ul>
  <li>High requirement volatility / high <strong>discovery</strong>.</li>
  <li>Need <strong>early time-to-value</strong> (e.g., <strong>MVP</strong>).</li>
  <li><strong>High market risk</strong> ‚Üí learn fast from feedback.</li>
  <li><strong>Self-managing team</strong> and engaged <strong>stakeholders</strong>.</li>
  <li>Work can be <strong>sliced</strong> into <strong>meaningful, valuable increments</strong>.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Avoid / go Hybrid when</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Heavy compliance/safety</strong> (healthcare, aviation‚Ä¶). <em>Tip:</em> consider <strong>Hybrid</strong> for R&amp;D/design.</li>
  <li><strong>Fixed scope/price</strong> contracts with limited change mechanisms.</li>
  <li><strong>Long, complex supply chains</strong> that hinder regular value increments.</li>
  <li><strong>Stakeholders not ready</strong> to provide regular feedback.</li>
</ul>

<h3>How to pick an approach</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Agile</strong>: when the goal is <strong>learn fast + change fast + deliver early value</strong>.</li>
  <li><strong>Hybrid</strong>: when <strong>compliance constraints</strong> exist but you still need <strong>experimentation/discovery</strong>.</li>
  <li><strong>Plan-driven</strong>: when <strong>scope is fixed</strong>, change is hard, and compliance risk is high.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Quick decision matrix</h3>

| Factor | Low | Medium | High |
|---|---|---|---|
| Requirement volatility | Predictive | Hybrid | Agile |
| Technical complexity | Predictive | Hybrid | Agile/Spikes |
| Compliance/Safety | Agile/Hybrid | Hybrid | Predictive/Hybrid |
| Early value need | Predictive | Hybrid | Agile |
| Increment slice-ability | Predictive | Hybrid | Agile |

<h2>3) Agile vs Predictive vs Hybrid</h2>

<h3>Predictive (Waterfall)</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Sequential</strong> approach: analysis ‚Üí design ‚Üí build ‚Üí test.</li>
  <li><strong>Up-front detailed planning</strong> with fixed phases.</li>
  <li>Best when <strong>requirements are stable</strong> and change is <strong>low</strong>.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Agile</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Iterative &amp; incremental</strong> approach.</li>
  <li>Work in <strong>Sprints</strong>; each iteration delivers a <strong>usable Increment</strong>.</li>
  <li>Best when <strong>requirements change often</strong> and you must <strong>learn fast</strong>.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Hybrid</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Combines</strong> Predictive and Agile per workstream.</li>
  <li>Example: <strong>Predictive</strong> for <strong>contracts/compliance</strong>; <strong>Agile</strong> for <strong>development or R&amp;D</strong>.</li>
  <li>Useful when you face both <strong>hard constraints</strong> and <strong>exploration/innovation</strong> needs.</li>
</ul>

| Aspect | Agile | Predictive (Waterfall) | Hybrid |
|---|---|---|---|
| Approach | Iterative &amp; incremental | Sequential, up-front plan | Contextual mix |
| Scope | Evolves | Fixed early | Partly fixed |
| Value | Delivered early via increments | End of project | Milestones + increments |
| Risk mgmt | Continuous, empirical | Front-loaded | Combined |
| Documentation | ‚ÄúJust enough‚Äù | Heavy | As compliance requires |
| Contracts | T&amp;M, value-based | Fixed scope/price | Milestones + increments |

<p><em>Tip:</em> Start with a <strong>risk-based Hybrid</strong>, then shift toward Agile as conditions allow.</p>

<h2>4) Agile methods in a nutshell</h2>

<h3>Scrum</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Widely used, lightweight framework based on <strong>empiricism</strong>.</li>
  <li><strong>Sprint</strong> length fixed, typically <strong>2‚Äì4 weeks</strong>.</li>
  <li>Roles: <strong>Product Owner</strong>, <strong>Scrum Master</strong>, <strong>Development Team</strong>.</li>
  <li>Events: <strong>Daily Scrum</strong>, <strong>Sprint Review</strong>, <strong>Sprint Retrospective</strong> (and <em>Sprint Planning</em>).</li>
</ul>

<h3>Kanban</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Optimizes <strong>flow</strong> by limiting <strong>WIP</strong> (Work In Progress).</li>
  <li>Visualized on a <strong>Kanban board</strong>; work is pulled across states.</li>
  <li><strong>Pull</strong> system; track <em>lead time</em>/<em>cycle time</em>; continuous improvement.</li>
</ul>

<h3>eXtreme Programming (XP)</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Emphasizes <strong>technical practices</strong> for quality &amp; adaptability.</li>
  <li>Key practices: <strong>pair programming</strong>, <strong>TDD/automated tests</strong>, <strong>continuous integration (CI)</strong>.</li>
  <li>Also: <em>refactoring</em>, <em>simple design</em>, <em>continuous feedback</em>.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Lean</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Inspired by the <strong>Toyota Production System</strong>.</li>
  <li>Goal: <strong>eliminate waste</strong> to <strong>maximize customer value</strong>.</li>
  <li>Principles: <em>flow</em>, <em>pull</em>, <em>kaizen</em> (continuous improvement).</li>
</ul>

| Method | One-liner | Use when | Avoid when |
|---|---|---|---|
| Scrum | 1‚Äì4 week Sprints, clear roles, review/retro | Digital products needing cadence &amp; transparency | Heavy dependencies block timeboxes |
| Kanban | Visual flow, WIP limits, flow metrics | Ops/Support, ad-hoc demand | Poor WIP discipline |
| XP | TDD, pairing, CI/CD, refactoring | High quality &amp; rapid change | Culture not ready for disciplined engineering |
| Lean | Remove waste, optimize flow | End-to-end value stream | ‚ÄúBlind cost-cutting‚Äù |
| Crystal | Tailor by size/criticality | Small‚Äìmid teams, comms-first | Org demands rigid standardization |
| DSDM | Timeboxing + MoSCoW | Clear time boundaries | Very large scale, heavy dependencies |
| FDD | Feature-driven development | Multi-domain enterprise | Need ultra-fast feedback |
| SAFe/LeSS | Scaling frameworks | Many teams to coordinate | Small orgs; cargo-cult adoption |
| Spotify Model | Squad/Tribe/Chapter/Guild | Strong empowerment culture | Copying without context |

<h2>5) FAQ (EN)</h2>

<h3>Does Agile completely replace Waterfall?</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>No</strong>. They are <strong>complementary</strong>, not oppositional.</li>
  <li>Many contexts benefit from <strong>Hybrid</strong>: stable parts run <em>Predictive</em>, evolving parts run <em>Agile</em>.</li>
</ul>

<h3>How is Scrum different from Kanban?</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Scrum</strong>: fixed-length <strong>Sprints</strong>, standardized <strong>roles</strong> &amp; <strong>events</strong>.</li>
  <li><strong>Kanban</strong>: <strong>flow</strong> optimization with <strong>WIP limits</strong>, <strong>no required Sprints</strong> or fixed roles.</li>
  <li>Pick <em>Scrum</em> for cadence/timeboxes; pick <em>Kanban</em> for continuous flow.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Does PMI-ACP require ‚Äúpure Agile‚Äù?</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>No</strong>. It emphasizes a <strong>flexible mindset</strong> and <strong>fit-for-context</strong> choices.</li>
  <li>Covers <strong>Scrum, Kanban, XP, Lean</strong> plus awareness of <strong>Predictive/Hybrid</strong>.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Do Agile projects need documentation?</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Yes</strong>, but <strong>‚Äújust enough‚Äù</strong> to deliver value &amp; meet requirements.</li>
  <li>Supports communication, quality, and <strong>compliance</strong>; updated iteratively across the lifecycle.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Can Agile be used on high-compliance projects?</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Often via <strong>Hybrid</strong>: <em>Agile</em> for discovery/design, <em>Predictive</em> for <strong>compliance</strong> phases.</li>
  <li>Combine controls such as <strong>QA/validation/traceability</strong> alongside Sprints.</li>
</ul>

<h2>6) Real-World Example: AI Virtual Assistant for an Enterprise</h2>

<h3>Project Context</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Build an <strong>AI virtual assistant</strong> to answer customer FAQs.</li>
  <li>Initial requirements are <strong>ambiguous</strong> and change quickly due to the pace of AI.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Applying Agile (Scrum/Kanban)</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Short loops &amp; learning</strong>: split work into <strong>2-week Sprints</strong> with early feedback.</li>
  <li><strong>Sprint 1</strong>: ship an <strong>MVP</strong> that answers ~10 basic questions.</li>
  <li><strong>Sprint 2</strong>: add pricing-related answers based on customer feedback.</li>
  <li><strong>Sprint 3</strong>: improve <strong>NLP</strong> and integrate with the internal <strong>CRM</strong>.</li>
  <li><strong>Responding to change</strong>: when a better <strong>LLM</strong> appears, plan a swap in the next Sprint.</li>
  <li><strong>Time-to-value</strong>: the customer can <strong>use it from Sprint 1</strong> instead of waiting six months.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Hybrid in the AI Context</h2>

<h3>Predictive (fixed)</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Contracts</strong>, <strong>security</strong>, and <strong>legal compliance</strong> (GDPR, CCPA) are planned in detail.</li>
  <li>Clear <strong>controls/traceability</strong> and stage gates for acceptance.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Agile (flexible)</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>R&amp;D</strong>, model training, and feature rollout via <strong>Sprints/Kanban</strong>.</li>
  <li>Optimize with <strong>feedback</strong>; try fast ‚Äì fail fast ‚Äì learn fast.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Outcomes</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Meets <strong>compliance &amp; contractual</strong> needs while keeping <strong>flexibility</strong> to track AI changes.</li>
  <li>Maximizes <strong>early value</strong> and <strong>reduces the risk</strong> of building the wrong thing.</li>
</ul>
